ğŸ° Castling
A special move involving the king and a rook.
Conditions:
Neither the king nor the rook has moved before.
No pieces between the king and rook.
The king is not in check, does not pass over a threatened square, and does not end up in check.
Short castling (king-side): king moves 2 squares to the right.
Long castling (queen-side): king moves 2 squares to the left.

ğŸ¯ Check and Checkmate
Check: your king is under attack. You must get out of check on your next move.
You can escape by:
Moving the king
Blocking the attack
Capturing the attacking piece
Checkmate: your king is in check and cannot escape. You lose the game.
ğŸ•Š Stalemate and Draws
A draw happens if:

The current player has no legal moves and is not in check (stalemate).
Insufficient material to checkmate (e.g. king vs king, or king + bishop vs king).
Threefold repetition of the same position.
50-move rule: if 50 consecutive moves occur with no capture or pawn move.
