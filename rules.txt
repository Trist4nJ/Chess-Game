♟ Official Chess Rules – Complete Version
––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

📌 Object of the Game
The goal is to checkmate your opponent’s king, meaning the king is under attack (in check) and there is no legal move to escape the threat.

––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

🧱 The Board
The game is played on an 8x8 square board.
Each player starts with 16 pieces:
1 King 👑
1 Queen 👸
2 Rooks 🏰
2 Bishops ⛪
2 Knights ♞
8 Pawns ♟
The board is positioned so that each player has a white/light square on their right.

––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

🔄 Turns
White always moves first, then players alternate turns.
You must make a move on your turn. Skipping a turn is not allowed.

––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

🧩 Piece Movement

👑 King
Moves one square in any direction (horizontal, vertical, diagonal).
Cannot move into or through a square that is under attack.
Special move: Castling (see below).

👸 Queen
Moves any number of squares in any direction (like rook + bishop).
Cannot jump over pieces.

🏰 Rook
Moves any number of squares vertically or horizontally.
Special move: Castling (see below).

⛪ Bishop
Moves any number of squares diagonally.
Always stays on the same color.

♞ Knight
Moves in an L-shape: two squares in one direction, then one perpendicular.
Can jump over pieces.

♟ Pawn
Moves forward one square.
Can move two squares forward on its first move.
Captures diagonally, not straight.
Special moves:
En passant: If an enemy pawn moves two squares forward next to yours, you may capture it as if it moved only one.
Promotion: If a pawn reaches the opposite end, it must be promoted to a queen, rook, bishop, or knight.

––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

🛡 Special Moves

🏰 Castling
A special move involving the king and a rook.
Conditions:
Neither the king nor the rook has moved before.
No pieces between the king and rook.
The king is not in check, does not pass over a threatened square, and does not end up in check.
Short castling (king-side): king moves 2 squares to the right.
Long castling (queen-side): king moves 2 squares to the left.

🎯 Check and Checkmate
Check: your king is under attack. You must get out of check on your next move.
You can escape by:
Moving the king
Blocking the attack
Capturing the attacking piece
Checkmate: your king is in check and cannot escape. You lose the game.
🕊 Stalemate and Draws
A draw happens if:

The current player has no legal moves and is not in check (stalemate).
Insufficient material to checkmate (e.g. king vs king, or king + bishop vs king).
Threefold repetition of the same position.
50-move rule: if 50 consecutive moves occur with no capture or pawn move.
🏁 Winning the Game

You win by checkmating your opponent.
You can also win if your opponent resigns.
A game can end in a draw.
